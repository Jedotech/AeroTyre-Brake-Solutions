Aircraft Brake Wear Limits Explained

Understanding Carbon Brake Life & Replacement Intervals

Aircraft brake systems experience extreme thermal loads during landing. Over time, brake discs wear down and eventually reach minimum allowable limits defined in the Aircraft Maintenance Manual (AMM).

How Brake Wear Occurs

Brake wear primarily occurs due to friction generated during landing and taxi operations. Each landing cycle removes a small amount of material from the brake stack.

Carbon vs Steel Brake Wear

  • Carbon Brakes – lighter weight and better heat absorption
  • Steel Brakes – lower cost but heavier and wear faster
  • Carbon brakes generally last more landing cycles

Minimum Wear Limits

Aircraft manufacturers define minimum brake stack thickness limits. Once the brake reaches this limit, it must be removed from service.

  • Brake stack thickness measurement
  • Heat cycle limits
  • Visual inspection for cracks
  • Disc wear indicators

Operational Factors Affecting Brake Life

  • Runway length
  • Landing weight
  • Taxi time
  • Brake cooling procedures
