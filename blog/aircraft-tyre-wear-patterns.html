Aircraft Tyre Wear Patterns

Understanding aircraft tyre wear patterns is essential for maintenance engineers and operators to prevent failures and ensure safe operations.

Aircraft tyres endure extreme loads, high landing speeds, and severe runway friction. Recognizing wear patterns early helps avoid costly AOG situations.

1. Centre Wear

This occurs when the tyre center tread wears faster than the shoulders.

  • Cause: Over-inflation
  • Impact: Reduced braking effectiveness
  • Solution: Maintain correct inflation pressure

2. Shoulder Wear

  • Cause: Under-inflation
  • Effect: Excess heat and structural stress
  • Prevention: Frequent pressure checks

3. Flat Spotting

Flat spots occur due to locked wheels during landing.

  • Usually caused by heavy braking
  • Common during rejected takeoffs
  • Requires immediate inspection

4. Rib Under-Cutting

One rib becomes thinner than others.

  • Often caused by wheel misalignment
  • May indicate landing gear alignment issues

5. Foreign Object Damage (FOD)

  • Cuts or gouges in tread
  • Caused by debris on runway

Best Practices for Tyre Life

  • Maintain proper inflation
  • Rotate tyres if applicable
  • Inspect after heavy landings
  • Monitor tread depth regularly
Proactive tyre monitoring significantly reduces unscheduled maintenance and improves fleet reliability.
