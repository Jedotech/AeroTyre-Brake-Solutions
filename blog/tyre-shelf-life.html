How Aircraft Tyre Shelf Life Affects Safety

Aviation Compliance | Structural Integrity | Risk Mitigation

Aircraft tyres are engineered to withstand extreme landing loads, temperature variations, and high-speed stress. However, even unused tyres have a defined shelf life that directly impacts operational safety.

What Is Aircraft Tyre Shelf Life?

Aircraft tyre shelf life refers to the maximum recommended storage period before a tyre is mounted and placed into service. Manufacturers typically define limits based on rubber compound composition and environmental storage conditions.

Proper storage in controlled humidity and temperature environments is critical to preserving structural integrity.

Safety Risks of Expired Tyres

  • Sidewall cracking due to rubber degradation
  • Reduced structural integrity under landing load
  • Tread separation risk
  • Increased blowout probability
