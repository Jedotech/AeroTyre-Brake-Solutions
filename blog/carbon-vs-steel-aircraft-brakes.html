Carbon vs Steel Aircraft Brakes

Performance, Weight & Lifecycle Comparison

Aircraft brake systems typically use either carbon or steel brake materials. Each option offers different operational advantages depending on aircraft type and mission profile.

Carbon Brakes

  • Significantly lighter weight
  • Higher heat absorption capacity
  • Longer service life
  • Higher acquisition cost

Steel Brakes

  • Lower purchase cost
  • Heavier system weight
  • Shorter service intervals

Operational Considerations

Modern commercial aircraft such as Airbus A320 and Boeing 737 typically use carbon brake technology due to its weight savings and improved performance.
