FAA 8130-3 vs EASA Form 1

Understanding Aviation Parts Certification Requirements

What is FAA Form 8130-3?

FAA Form 8130-3 is an Authorized Release Certificate issued under U.S. Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) regulations. It certifies that a part or component meets approved airworthiness standards and is eligible for installation on certified aircraft.

What is EASA Form 1?

EASA Form 1 is the European equivalent certificate issued under European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) regulations. It confirms compliance with European airworthiness requirements.

Why Certification Matters

  • Ensures regulatory airworthiness compliance
  • Required for installation on certified aircraft
  • Mandatory for international export transactions
  • Provides traceability and documentation history

Can One Certificate Replace the Other?

Under certain bilateral aviation safety agreements between authorities, dual-release certificates may be accepted. However, operators must always verify with their local aviation authority before installation.
