Aircraft Brake Systems Explained

Aircraft braking systems are critical for safe landing and taxi operations.

Modern aircraft brakes must absorb enormous kinetic energy during landing — often equivalent to several megajoules.

Main Components

  • Brake discs
  • Hydraulic actuators
  • Anti-skid system
  • Brake control unit

Steel vs Carbon Brakes

Steel Brakes

  • Lower cost
  • Heavier
  • Common on smaller aircraft

Carbon Brakes

  • Much lighter
  • Better heat resistance
  • Longer life cycles

Anti-Skid System

Aircraft use anti-skid systems similar to automotive ABS systems to prevent wheel lock during braking.

Common Brake Issues

  • Overheating
  • Uneven wear
  • Hydraulic leaks
  • Disc cracks
Proper brake inspections prevent runway overruns and extend brake service life.
