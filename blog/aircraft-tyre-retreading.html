Aircraft Tyre Retreading

Retreading is a common practice in aviation to extend tyre life and reduce operating costs.

Some aircraft tyres can be retreaded up to 7 times depending on casing condition.

Retreading Process

  • Initial inspection
  • Buffing worn tread
  • Applying new rubber
  • Vulcanization
  • Final inspection

Benefits

  • Cost savings
  • Environmental sustainability
  • Extended tyre lifecycle

Limitations

  • Only possible if casing is intact
  • Requires certified retread facilities
