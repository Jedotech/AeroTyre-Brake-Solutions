Aircraft Tyre Pressure Safety Guide

Why Correct Inflation Prevents Failures

Aircraft tyres operate at extremely high pressures compared to automotive tyres. Maintaining correct inflation is critical for structural integrity and safe landing performance.

Why Aircraft Tyres Use Nitrogen

  • Stable pressure under temperature changes
  • Reduces moisture inside the tyre
  • Minimizes oxidation of internal components

Risks of Underinflation

  • Sidewall flexing and overheating
  • Premature tread wear
  • Higher probability of tyre failure

Risks of Overinflation

  • Reduced tyre contact area
  • Uneven tread wear
  • Higher landing impact stress

Best Maintenance Practices

  • Daily pressure checks
  • Use calibrated gauges
  • Monitor pressure trends across fleet
