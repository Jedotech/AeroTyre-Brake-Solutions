Aircraft Tyre Pressure Guide

Maintaining correct tyre pressure is critical for aircraft safety and tyre longevity.

Aircraft tyres typically operate between 180 PSI and 220 PSI depending on aircraft type.

Why Pressure Matters

  • Prevents structural tyre damage
  • Improves braking performance
  • Reduces uneven wear

Under-Inflation Risks

  • Excess heat buildup
  • Sidewall fatigue
  • Potential tyre failure

Over-Inflation Risks

  • Reduced tread contact
  • Uneven center wear
  • Higher stress during landing

Best Practices

  • Daily pressure checks
  • Use calibrated gauges
  • Adjust for ambient temperature
