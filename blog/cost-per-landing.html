Aircraft Tyre Cost Per Landing Explained

Optimizing Operating Cost Through Tyre Lifecycle Management

What is Cost Per Landing?

Cost per landing (CPL) is calculated by dividing the total tyre acquisition cost by the number of safe landings completed before removal or retread. It is one of the most important metrics for airline maintenance and procurement teams.

Example Calculation

If a tyre costs ₹1,50,000 and safely completes 250 landings:

₹1,50,000 ÷ 250 = ₹600 per landing

Lower CPL indicates better operational efficiency — but durability, retread capability, and performance must also be considered.

Factors That Affect Tyre Life

  • Runway surface condition
  • Landing technique and braking intensity
  • Aircraft weight at touchdown
  • Proper tyre pressure maintenance
  • Brake heat exposure

How Operators Reduce Cost Per Landing

  • Routine pressure monitoring programs
  • Structured inspection intervals
  • Maximizing approved retread cycles
  • Correct storage procedures before installation
  • Strategic sourcing from verified suppliers
