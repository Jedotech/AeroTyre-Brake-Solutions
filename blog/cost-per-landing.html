Aircraft Tyre Cost Per Landing Explained

Optimizing Operating Cost Through Tyre Lifecycle Management

What is Cost Per Landing?

Cost per landing is calculated by dividing the total tyre purchase price by the number of safe landings completed before replacement.

Example Calculation

If a tyre costs ₹1,50,000 and lasts 250 landings:

₹1,50,000 ÷ 250 = ₹600 per landing

Factors That Affect Tyre Life

  • Runway surface condition
  • Landing technique
  • Aircraft weight
  • Tyre pressure maintenance
  • Brake heat exposure

How Operators Reduce Cost

  • Proper inflation checks
  • Routine inspection programs
  • Retread utilization
  • Correct storage procedures
